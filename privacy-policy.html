Data Handling

Privacy Policy

This policy explains what information is collected, how it is used for moderation and account operation, and how users may request correction, review, or removal where appropriate.

1. Data Collected

The platform may collect account and report-related data necessary to operate a moderated incident registry.

  • account details such as name, email, role, and server name where applicable
  • report submission data, including incident descriptions, screenshots, proof links, player names, UUIDs, and related metadata
  • moderation metadata such as review outcomes, timestamps, and audit rationale

2. localStorage Use

The front-end may use localStorage for session continuity, lightweight interface state, and client-side preferences. localStorage remains on the user’s device and may be cleared by the user at any time.

3. Purpose of Processing

  • operate user accounts and sign-in flows
  • receive, review, and moderate reports
  • maintain internal audit logs for moderation integrity
  • support appeals, corrections, and challenged content review

4. Public vs Internal Information

Not all submitted data is displayed publicly. The platform may hide reporter identity from public-facing cards while preserving internal records for moderation, appeals, and compliance purposes.

5. Review and Removal Requests

Users may request review, correction, or removal of specific records where there is error, duplication, mistaken identity, or newly relevant information. Such requests are handled through the platform’s moderation and appeals procedures.

6. Retention Guidance

For operational and dispute-resolution reasons, the platform should retain the original report, evidence submitted, moderation decision rationale, and related logs for a reasonable internal period, subject to later review or removal policies.

Privacy Baseline

  • limit public exposure where unnecessary
  • retain internal evidence for audit and appeals
  • avoid publication of unsupported allegations

Legal Disclaimer

Records shown on the service represent user-submitted reports assessed under moderation standards. Visibility of a record does not represent a definitive legal or factual finding of guilt.
