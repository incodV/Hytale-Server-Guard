Terms of Use

These terms position Hytale Server Guard as a moderated incident registry and community review platform. The service does not act as a court, public authority, or definitive adjudicator of guilt.

1. Nature of the Platform

Hytale Server Guard operates as a moderated repository of player reports, server incident records, and watchlist entries submitted by users and reviewed by administrators. Records are published only after human review and according to internal moderation standards.

Publication on the platform does not constitute a final legal finding, judicial determination, or definitive declaration of guilt.

2. User Responsibility

Each reporting user is solely responsible for the accuracy, lawfulness, and good-faith basis of the information submitted. Users must not submit fabricated, malicious, fraudulent, defamatory, or intentionally misleading allegations.

  • Reports must be supported by evidence.
  • False or retaliatory submissions are prohibited.
  • Users must not impersonate others or alter evidence to create misleading impressions.

3. Moderation and Editorial Rights

The platform reserves the right, at its discretion and in accordance with its policies, to review, reject, edit, limit, remove, archive, or suppress submitted records and related materials.

  • reject reports that do not satisfy minimum evidence standards
  • edit records for clarity, neutrality, or policy compliance
  • remove contested materials pending review
  • suspend or terminate abusive accounts
  • preserve internal moderation logs for audit purposes

4. No Automatic Publication

No report is published automatically. Every record is subject to human moderation before it becomes visible in the public Incident Registry or Moderated Watchlist.

5. Community Safe Harbor Principle

Hytale Server Guard acts as an intermediary for user-submitted content and reserves the right to review, restrict, or remove materials that are disputed, incomplete, or inconsistent with internal standards.

6. Abuse of the System

Use of the platform for harassment, retaliation, false reporting, or malicious escalation may result in account suspension, content deletion, denial of future submissions, and broader restriction of access.

Operational Rule

The public registry should prefer neutral expressions such as “reported by”, “incident recorded”, and “submission under review”. Avoid accusatory labels or definitive criminal language.
