Moderation Standards

Moderation Policy

This policy governs what may be reported, the minimum evidence threshold, and the review standards applied before any record appears in the public Player Reports or Moderated Watchlist.

1. Reportable Conduct

The platform may review submissions related to harmful conduct in community-operated Hytale servers, including but not limited to:

  • griefing and deliberate destruction of player or server assets
  • use of exploits or unauthorized mechanics abuse
  • scam attempts, fraud patterns, or deceptive trade behavior
  • hacks, cheats, or unauthorized client modifications
  • ban evasion and repeat-entry behavior after server sanctions
  • other harmful conduct that materially affects server safety or trust

2. Evidence Requirements

Every submission must contain clear supporting material. A report without evidence is not eligible for publication.

  • evidence is mandatory for review
  • links must be accessible and reasonably verifiable
  • allegations must be consistent with the materials submitted
  • records should identify the relevant player, server, and incident context

3. Human Review Process

No report is published automatically. Each submission undergoes human moderation before visibility is granted.

  • initial intake and completeness check
  • evidence review and consistency screening
  • neutral wording review and redaction where appropriate
  • approval, rejection, or hold for additional clarification

4. Rejection Criteria

Moderators should reject or suppress records that do not satisfy the minimum standard. Rejection may apply where a report is:

  • unsupported by evidence
  • backed by weak, ambiguous, or insufficient proof
  • duplicative without meaningful new elements
  • submitted in bad faith or for harassment
  • framed in defamatory or excessively accusatory language
  • inconsistent with the evidence shown

5. Internal Audit Recommendation

For internal compliance and dispute handling, the platform should retain the original submission, supporting evidence, moderation rationale, and operational logs showing who reviewed the case and when.

Mandatory Rules

  • Never publish without evidence.
  • Never publish automatically.
  • Always prefer neutral wording.

Terminology Guidance

Prefer terms such as Incident Registry, Player Reports, and Moderated Watchlist. Avoid dramatic or definitive labels suggesting criminal certainty.
