Review Rights

Appeals Policy

Players identified in a listed record may request review, correction, clarification, or removal where new evidence or material inaccuracies are presented.

1. Right to Request Review

A listed player may request a new moderation review of a published or unpublished record. Appeals may be used to challenge factual accuracy, context, identity matching, evidence quality, or continued publication.

2. Types of Appeal Requests

  • review of the evidence originally considered
  • correction of names, UUIDs, dates, or server references
  • submission of new contextual or exculpatory material
  • request for removal based on error, duplication, or resolved dispute

3. Basic Appeals Flow

  • identify the specific record or incident entry
  • explain the reason for the challenge in neutral, factual terms
  • provide new evidence or clarifying information where available
  • await human moderation review and outcome notice

The platform may request additional information before making a decision.

4. Potential Outcomes

  • record maintained without change
  • record corrected or narrowed
  • record temporarily hidden pending further review
  • record removed from public view or deleted internally where justified

5. Neutrality Standard

Appeals are reviewed as moderation matters, not as judicial findings. The platform should apply neutral language and avoid framing an appeal result as proof of innocence or guilt.

What Helps an Appeal

  • specific case identifier
  • clear explanation of what is inaccurate
  • new screenshots, logs, or server context
  • evidence of mistaken identity or duplication

False Report Protection

If an appeal shows malicious or fabricated use of the system, the original reporting account may face restriction, removal, or further moderation sanctions.
